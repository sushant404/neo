The World Bank in Nepal: Towards New Horizons

Dr. Paradox

Abstract

  • The World Bank in Nepal: Towards New Horizons offers a comprehensive, multidimensional examination of the World Bank's engagement in Nepal over the past five decades, presenting an assessment of development effectiveness. It explores how significant investments have transformed infrastructure, energy, and social sectors, while persistent challenges such as inequality, weak governance, and capacity constraints continue to limit sustainability.
  • Findings reveal notable improvements in connectivity and service delivery, yet gaps in quality, equity, and long-term resilience persist. The narrative highlights both successes and shortcomings, offering granular insights that underscore the need for adaptive governance and inclusive growth.
  • A comparative analysis of the past and new CPF (Country Partnership Framework, 2025-31) reveals a growing emphasis on climate adaptation, digital transformation, job creation and participatory processes. The recommendations stress local capacity-building, adaptive safety nets, systemic monitoring, and evidence-based policymaking.
  • Lessons drawn from across sectors serve as replicable models for other low-income and transitional economies pursuing equitable growth. At the same time, the book warns against recurring poor practices, including elite capture, inadequate contextualization, and fragmented implementation, that threaten to undermine development impact.
  • The book calls for more substantial alignment between vision and execution, deeper regional integration, and operational coherence. Through a synthesis of theory, data, and grounded practice, this volume moves beyond celebration and calls for bold, transformative reform and a co-created partnership model designed for Nepal's future.
